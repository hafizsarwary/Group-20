Bellinzona, the capital of the Swiss canton of Ticino, is situated near the Italian border and is known for its medieval castles and alpine scenery. The region's geological composition, characterized by steep slopes and diverse rock types, makes it susceptible to landslides, especially during periods of heavy rainfall or rapid snowmelt. A notable event occurred in May 2012 near Preonzo, approximately 20 kilometers from Bellinzona, where a significant landslide released around 300,000 cubic meters of rock. This event underscored the area's vulnerability to such natural hazards. To mitigate risks, local authorities have implemented monitoring systems and early warning measures to protect residents and infrastructure.

About the study area and Work Methodology.

The project covers 47 square kilometers with a total population of 2,364 people, among whom 97 individuals reside in a very high-risk landslide area. The study is based on accurate and reliable data, utilizing QGIS and advanced machine learning algorithms to achieve the highest possible accuracy in landslide susceptibility mapping.

To ensure precision, sample points were carefully selected and processed to train the algorithms, optimizing their performance in predicting hazardous zones. The final model produced an output accuracy of 82.75%, indicating a strong and reliable assessment of landslide-prone areas.

This approach enhances disaster risk management, helping authorities and planners make informed decisions to protect communities, infrastructure, and the environment..
